Standard Curriculum

Kinder (3-6) Years

Kindergarten, typically for children aged 3-6, is a crucial stage of early childhood education. It focuses on building foundational skills like language, literacy, math, social-emotional development, and physical development.

Elementary School

Elementary school, also known as primary school in some countries, is the first stage of formal education. It typically covers grades 1-5 or 6, depending on the education system. During these years, children learn fundamental skills in reading, writing, math, science, and social studies.

Middle (10-16) Years

The middle years, roughly ages 10 to 16, are a time of significant change and growth. It's a period of physical, cognitive, and social-emotional development, often referred to as adolescence. During this time, individuals experience rapid physical changes, including growth spurts and the onset of puberty.
